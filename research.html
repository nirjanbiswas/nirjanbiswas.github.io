My research has been in the analysis of PDE, more specifically in the elliptic PDE.




Doctoral Research

In the following, I briefly describe some of my doctoral research areas:

Generalized Hardy-Sobolev inequlaity and it’s application: One part of my thesis is devoted to studying the Hardy-Sobolev inequality. This inequality is one of the most well known in the area of Partial differential equations and it’s related fields. This inequality tells that for a smooth function f with compact support, the L^p norm of f/|x| is bounded by the L^p norm of the gradient of f. In the literature, an ample number of research have been done to improve this inequality in several directions. One can read this monograph: The Hardy Inequality. About Its History and Some Related Results to know about Hardy inequality from the beginning. My interest is to generalize this inequality with weights in various function spaces, for example, in Lorentz space, Lorentz-Zygmund space, Orlicz space. These are multi parameter family of function spaces that refine the classical Lebesgue space. In order to define and study the rearrangement invariant function spaces one needs to understand: the symmetrization of a function. There are some well-known inequalities involving symmetrization, for example, Hardy-Littlewood inequality and Pólya–Szegő inequality to name a few. Some useful references for this topic are the following:

  • Hardy Operators, Function Spaces and Embeddings (by David E. Edmunds and W. Desmond Evans)

  • Some New Functional Spaces (by G. G. Lorentz)

  • Sharp sobolev embeddings and related Hardy inequalities: The critical case (by David E. Edmunds and Hans Triebel)

One good motivation to generalize the inequality in this direction is that the weight functions in the classical Hardy inequality lie in these spaces (depending on the dimension). My thesis also studies other classes of weighted Hardy-Sobolev inequalities where the test functions do not vanish on the boundary of the domain.

The other part of my thesis focuses on finding functions spaces for which the best constant of the weighted Hardy-Sobolev inequality is attained. We provide a general sufficient condition on the weight function so that the best constant is attained. To be precise, the attainment of the best constant is equivalent to find a weak solution of the associated weighted eigenvalue problems for the p-Laplace operator with zero Dirichlet, Neumann, and Steklov boundary conditions. As a bi-product, I study these problems with a general class of weight functions.

Bifurcation of eigenvalues: The complete spectrum of the p-Laplace operator is not known. But it is well known that the spectrum consists of an increasing sequence of eigenvalues tending to infinity. An eigenvalue`a’ of the p-Laplace operator is called a bifurcation point (from zero) if there exists a sequence of pairs (a_n, v_n) of the perturbed eigenvalue problem such that the eigenvalues (a_n) converges to `a’, and the associated weak solutions (v_n) converges to 0 in the appropriate Sobolev space. There are many literatures studying the existence of bifurcation point for p-Laplace operator with Dirichlet boundary conditions. It is proved that the first positive eigenvalue is the bifurcation point for p-Laplace operator. In 1971, Paul Rabinowitz established an interesting fact for a general class of elliptic problems, which says that there exists an uncountable set of non-trivial weak solutions bifurcating from (a,0), such that the set is either unbounded or meets at (b,0), where `b’ is another eigenvalue of the p-Laplace operator. Further, depending on the multiplicity of `a’, this continuum decompose into two subcontinua of non-trivial solutions.

My research aims to establish the analogous results for the weighted eigenvalue problem of p-Laplace operator with Neumann and Steklov boundary condition and also with general weight functions in the Lorentz and Lorentz-Zygmud spaces. One of the most important tool in this analysis is the topological degree theory (for example, Leray–Schauder degree) for certain class of maps defined on the solution space. Some useful references for this topic are as follows:

  • Some global results for nonlinear eigenvalue problems (by Rabinowitz)

  • Methods for Analysis of Nonlinear Elliptic Boundary Value Problems (by I. V. Skrypnik)



Post-doctoral Research

My post doctoral research includes the following topics:

(a) Optimization of the first Dirichlet eigenvalue over certain rearrangement invariant classes.

(b) Positivity of the solutions on the entire R^N for some semipositone problems.

(c) Shape optimization of the fractional eigenvalues.

(d) The study of spectrum for inhomogeneous fractional operators.
